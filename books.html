Reccomended Readings on Personal Finance

It is best practice to read multiple books, this will both inform you and strengthen your resolve when it comes to saving, if you want to start with one, the Simple Path to Qealth and Retire Before Mom and Dad are good choices.

The Simple Path to Wealth

J.L. Collins

Edition: June 18 2016

Great first book to read, author is engaging and covers a lot of ground in this book.

The Automatic Millionaire

David Bach

Edition: 2016

This book is great! Making everything automatic when it comes to personal finance is a wonderful idea and perhaps the only one that will work for most people. Inspires action and makes it simple. The one thing I don't like is the way he breaks down asset allocation which is too complicated for most people. Listen to the other books about asset allocation, but besides that everything is great.

Retire Before Mom and Dad

Rob Berger

Edition: 2019

Great first book. But J.L. Collin's book is probably a little better as a first book. The math in this book is very informative about how small differences in savings rates can make a big difference later in life. A must read!

The Little Book on Common Sense Investing

John "Jack" Bogle

Edition: 2017

If you aren't sold on index funds, this book provides a lot of evidence towards their merit. Great book for those skeptical about index fund investing through ETF's and mutual funds. Great book for all investors, but a little dry sometimes. Good as a second or third book.

Bogleheads Guide to Investing

Tyler Larimore, Mel

Edition: 2014

Very detailed book, but a little dry. I would not reccomend as a first book, as if you do not know a about personal finance and individual types of investments, you may feel lost as many things are covered quickly.

Mortgage Free!

Rob Roy

Edition: 2nd

Dark horse book, reccomended for anyone. Best for the committed serious investor and is mostly a real estate book, not a traditional personal finance book. Buy a used copy on Amazon to save some bucks!
